About

I'm a graduate industrial chemical engineer working as a sales engineer — which means I've spent time on both sides of the purchase order: designing and troubleshooting process equipment, and helping clients specify, justify, and buy it.

I understand mass and energy balances well enough to catch a mis-sized pump on a datasheet, and I understand a buyer's priorities well enough to know that spec sheet won't close the deal on its own. That combination is what I bring to every project: technically credible, commercially useful.

Outside of client meetings, I like getting back into the weeds — process simulations, P&ID reviews, and finding the cheaper equivalent spec that still meets the process requirement.

My Story

My name is Kipkorir Kevin Isack, born in the year 2004 in the serene Kilibwoni Village of Nandi County, Kenya. This autobiography is not merely a recount of my achievements or a chronology of events but a journey through the experiences that have shaped me into who I am today. From the modest beginnings in a rural Kenyan village to the academic halls of Dedan Kimathi University of Technology, my life has been a testament to the power of education, resilience, and the unwavering support of a loving family. It is a narrative of overcoming challenges, embracing opportunities, and the relentless pursuit of knowledge.

Growing up in Kilibwoni provided me with a unique perspective on life. The rolling hills and vibrant community instilled in me a deep appreciation for nature and a sense of belonging within my cultural heritage. Family gatherings, filled with laughter and storytelling, fostered bonds that would endure through the years, shaping my values of togetherness, respect, and resilience.

From a young age, my curiosity knew no bounds. Inspired by my parents' emphasis on education and self-improvement, I eagerly delved into a world of learning and exploration. My early dreams reflected this spirit of curiosity and ambition.

Initially drawn to the realm of law, I was captivated by the principles of justice and fairness. This aspiration, rooted in a desire to make a positive impact, set the stage for my journey of self-discovery.

As I matured, my interests expanded to encompass the field of healthcare. Witnessing the challenges faced by communities in accessing quality medical care ignited a passion within me to become a healer. The dream of becoming a doctor, of alleviating pain and restoring hope, became a driving force in my academic pursuits.

Yet, the allure of engineering beckoned with promises of innovation and problem-solving. I found myself drawn to the idea of designing solutions to complex challenges, of pushing the boundaries of what was possible. This fascination with technology and creativity fueled my imagination and opened new pathways of exploration.

Amidst these evolving aspirations, I remained anchored by the values instilled in me by my upbringing. The importance of hard work, integrity, and empathy guided my actions and decisions, shaping my character and outlook on life. I embraced challenges as opportunities for growth, learning valuable lessons along the way.

It was during my educational journey that I discovered Industrial Chemistry — a discipline that combined my love for science, creativity, and real-world application. The blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills resonated deeply with my interests and aspirations, leading me to pursue a degree in this field.

Today, I stand at the threshold of a promising future. My journey from childhood dreams to current pursuits reflects a trajectory shaped by resilience, curiosity, and a deep-rooted passion for making a meaningful difference in the world.

Career and Work Experience

After completing high school, I embarked on a period of valuable experiential learning that significantly contributed to my understanding of practical applications in chemistry. While awaiting admission to university, I took on a tutoring role at a local high school — an experience that refined my communication skills, reinforced my understanding of academic concepts, and fostered a passion for teaching and knowledge-sharing.

Simultaneously, I undertook an attachment at the Ministry of Water laboratories — a pivotal experience that allowed me to gain hands-on experience conducting water quality tests and analyses. Working under the guidance of experienced professionals, I learned to perform pH testing, turbidity measurements, chemical analyses for contaminants, and microbial assessments.

The attachment provided a comprehensive overview of the critical role of chemistry in ensuring water safety and quality. I gained practical insights into the methodologies, protocols, and standards employed in water testing, developing proficiency in using laboratory equipment, interpreting test results, and adhering to stringent quality control measures.

One of the highlights was contributing to real-world projects focused on water quality assessment — from assessing drinking water quality in rural communities to evaluating wastewater treatment processes in urban areas. These projects deepened my technical skills and enhanced my understanding of the broader implications of chemistry in addressing societal challenges related to water resources management.

Through this hands-on experience, I honed critical skills such as attention to detail, data analysis, problem-solving, and collaboration within multidisciplinary teams. I collaborated with environmental scientists, engineers, and policymakers on initiatives aimed at improving water treatment processes and developing sustainable water management strategies.

This attachment instilled in me a strong sense of environmental responsibility and ethical conduct — the importance of adhering to ethical guidelines, maintaining data integrity, and prioritizing transparency in scientific reporting. It also enhanced my leadership skills, adaptability, and resilience in dynamic work environments, reinforcing my commitment to pursuing a career where I can apply my knowledge and skills to make meaningful contributions to society, particularly in environmental sustainability, public health, and scientific innovation.
